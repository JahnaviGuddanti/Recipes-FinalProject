Sambar Recipe


Ingredients:

  • For the Sambar:
  • 1 cup toor dal (split pigeon peas)
  • 2 cups mixed vegetables (carrot, potato, eggplant, okra, drumstick, etc.)
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit
  • 1 tablespoon tamarind pulp
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 2 tablespoons sambar powder
  • Salt to taste
  • Water as needed
  • For the Tempering:
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon fenugreek seeds
  • 1 dried red chili, broken into pieces
  • A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
  • 10-12 curry leaves
  • Garnish:
  • Fresh coriander leaves, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Dal:

    Wash the toor dal thoroughly in water until the water runs clear. In a pressure cooker, add the washed dal and 2-3 cups of water. Add 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder and a few drops of oil (to prevent frothing). Close the lid and pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or until the dal is soft and fully cooked. Once the pressure releases, mash the cooked dal well and set aside.

  2. Prepare the Tamarind Pulp:

    Soak a small lemon-sized ball of tamarind in warm water for about 10 minutes. Squeeze the tamarind to extract the pulp and strain to remove any seeds or fibers. Set aside.

  3. Cook the Vegetables:

    In a large pot, add the mixed vegetables, chopped onion, chopped tomato, green chilies, and a pinch of turmeric powder. Add enough water to cover the vegetables and bring to a boil. Cook the vegetables until they are tender but not mushy.

  4. Make the Sambar:

    Once the vegetables are cooked, add the mashed toor dal to the pot. Add the tamarind pulp, sambar powder, and salt to taste. Mix well and bring to a boil. Adjust the consistency by adding water if needed. Let the sambar simmer for 10-15 minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.

  5. Tempering:

    In a small pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add mustard seeds and let them splutter. Add cumin seeds, fenugreek seeds, dried red chili, asafoetida, and curry leaves. Saut√© for a few seconds until the spices are fragrant. Pour the tempering over the simmering sambar and mix well.

  6. Garnish and Serve:

    Garnish the sambar with freshly chopped coriander leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, idli, dosa, or any South Indian dish of your choice.
