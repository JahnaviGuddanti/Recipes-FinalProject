Spicy Mushroom Biryani Recipe

Ingredients:

  • Basmati rice: 1 cup, soaked for 30 minutes and drained
  • Mushrooms: 200 grams, cleaned and sliced
  • Onion: 2 large, thinly sliced
  • Tomato: 2 medium, chopped
  • Green chilies: 2-3, slit
  • Ginger-garlic paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Yogurt: 1/4 cup
  • Biryani masala: 2 teaspoons
  • Red chili powder: 1 teaspoon (adjust to taste)
  • Turmeric powder: 1/2 teaspoon
  • Cumin seeds: 1 teaspoon
  • Cinnamon stick: 1-inch piece
  • Cloves: 3-4
  • Cardamom pods: 2-3
  • Bay leaf: 1
  • Salt: to taste
  • Oil: 3 tablespoons
  • Ghee (clarified butter): 1 tablespoon
  • Fresh coriander leaves: for garnish
  • Fried onions: for garnish (optional)
  • Saffron strands: a pinch (dissolved in 2 tablespoons of warm milk)

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the Rice:
    • Boil 4 cups of water in a large pot.
    • Add salt, whole spices (cumin seeds, cinnamon, cloves, cardamom pods, bay leaf) to the water.
    • Add drained rice and cook until the rice is 70-80% cooked (about 7-8 minutes).
    • Drain the rice and set aside.
  2. Prepare the Mushrooms:
    • Heat 1 tablespoon of oil in a pan.
    • Add sliced mushrooms and sauté until they are golden brown and cooked through.
    • Remove from the pan and set aside.
  3. Prepare the Gravy:
    • In the same pan, heat the remaining oil and ghee.
    • Add sliced onions and sauté until golden brown.
    • Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies. Sauté for another 2 minutes until the raw smell disappears.
    • Add chopped tomatoes and cook until they turn soft and oil starts separating.
    • Add biryani masala, red chili powder, turmeric powder, and salt. Mix well and cook for 2-3 minutes.
    • Add yogurt and cook for another 2 minutes until the mixture thickens slightly.
  4. Layering:
    • In a heavy-bottomed pan or biryani pot, layer half of the cooked rice.
    • Spread half of the mushroom mixture over the rice.
    • Sprinkle half of the saffron milk over the mushrooms.
    • Layer the remaining rice over the mushrooms.
    • Spread the remaining mushrooms over the rice.
    • Drizzle the remaining saffron milk over the top.
    • Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and fried onions (if using).
  5. Dum Cooking:
    • Cover the pan tightly with a lid. You can also seal the edges with dough (optional) to trap steam.
    • Cook on low heat (dum) for 15-20 minutes until the rice is fully cooked and aromatic.
  6. Serve:
    • Once done, gently mix the layers before serving to distribute the flavors evenly.
    • Serve hot with raita (yogurt sauce) and your favorite salad.
